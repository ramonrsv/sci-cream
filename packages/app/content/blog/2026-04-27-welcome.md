# Welcome to Sci-Cream

## Background

Back in 2024, I bought my first ice cream machine on a whim - I'd always loved ice cream and wanted
to try making my own. Just following some recipes online went reasonably well. Trying to create my
own was an unmitigated disaster - I made a "protein" ice cream that had the consistency of tar. So I
did some research, which led me to Dream Scoop's wonderful [Ice Cream
Science](https://www.dreamscoops.com/ice-cream-science/) article and their [ice cream calculator
spreadsheet](https://www.dreamscoops.com/ice-cream-science/ice-cream-calculator/). This was a
game-changer, and my first introduction to ice cream science.

I was curious about the math going on underneath, so I started digging through the formulas,
eventually expanding the functionality and making it my own. The spreadsheet was later ported to
Google Sheets, where it eventually morphed into a fully-featured Apps Script library with 2k+ lines
of code:

![Apps Script Ice Cream Calculator](/images/blog/apps-script-ice-cream-calculator.png)

At some point in the middle of that, I discovered the fantastic
[icecreamcalc.com](https://icecreamcalc.com/). It did everything I was trying to do and then some. I
no longer had a _need_ for my app, but I liked the convenience of it being online. The deployed
library could also be added to any spreadsheet for convenient mix properties summaries, and it had
some other nifty features, like parametric ingredients `Strawberry [Brix 9]`, so I kept using it.

<img src="/images/blog/apps-script-mix-summary.png" alt="Apps Script Mix Summary" width="400"
style="display: block; margin: 0 auto;">

Ever the nerd, I found the science and software development aspects interesting, so I continued
digging into it, finding more resources, experimenting with my own recipes, improving the app, etc.
Eventually, the Apps Script setup became a real limitation, so I decided to port it into a proper
project. A year or so later, here is _Sci-Cream_!

## Calculator

First and foremost is the [Sci-Cream Calculator](/calculator), meant to be a fully-featured ice
cream recipe calculator, but with the accessibility that comes with being web-based. There are
[other ice cream calculator applications](/docs/resources#calculators) available, but I find that
they are either too simple, like the various online Excel spreadsheets, or they require local
software installations. The latter often have poor cross-platform support, or require payment,
sometimes at exorbitant prices well beyond the reach of home cooks.

![Sci-Cream Calculator](/images/blog/sci-cream-calculator-half-screen.png)

My humble hope is that the Sci-Cream Calculator will change that - providing the depth of
functionality one would expect from a paid desktop application, yet accessible to anyone with a
device and an internet connection. At the moment, it is far from that, but it's early days. A
snippet from the [`app-v0.0.1`](https://github.com/ramonrsv/sci-cream/releases/tag/app-v0.0.1)
release:

<img src="/images/blog/sci-cream-app-release-0.0.1.png" alt="sci-cream App Release 0.0.1"
width="600" style="display: block; margin: 0 auto;">

I hope to continue adding functionality, some of which could help foster knowledge sharing and
discussion about ice cream science and recipes. Some highlights of upcoming features from
[`TODO.md`](https://github.com/ramonrsv/sci-cream/blob/7539442/packages/app/TODO.md):

- Recipe save/store functionality with notes, version control, inter-recipe links, etc.
- Recipe share functionality, producing shareable links, which can also be embedded in sites.
- Nutrition Facts Table generation - the information is already tracked by the Rust library.
- Support for user-defined ingredients, including sharing functionality similar to recipes'.
- App tour and User-Guide documentation, detailing the website's functionality and how to use it.
- Support for automatic recipe and ingredient set balancing - Rust library has preliminary support.

## Open-source Library

When diving into the world of ice cream science and calculators, I found it very frustrating that I
could not see what the various software packages were doing in the background. I wanted Sci-Cream to
change that, so everything is freely available and [open
source](https://github.com/ramonrsv/sci-cream).

<img src="/images/blog/sci-cream-github-page.png" alt="Sci-Cream GitHub Page" width="800"
style="display: block; margin: 0 auto;">

Most notably, the core of the software is the [`sci-cream` Rust
crate](https://crates.io/crates/sci-cream). Anyone can use this library in their own application,
which handles all the math and ice cream science calculations.

- It supports native Rust, as well as web applications via WebAssembly (that's how the [Sci-Cream
  Calculator](/calculator) uses it).
- The [ingredient
  database](https://github.com/ramonrsv/sci-cream/tree/main/packages/sci-cream/data/ingredients) is
  public and includes detailed descriptions of how values were calculated for every ingredient.
- Specs provide a user-friendly way to define new ingredients, with ample [documentation and
  examples](https://docs.rs/sci-cream/latest/sci_cream/specs/index.html).
- In addition to inline documentation, there is a section on general [ice cream science
  knowledge](https://docs.rs/sci-cream/latest/sci_cream/docs/index.html).

At the time of writing, the library already has most of the basic ice cream science functionality.
From the [`0.0.1`](https://github.com/ramonrsv/sci-cream/releases/tag/sci-cream-v0.0.1) release:

<img src="/images/blog/sci-cream-release-0.0.1.png" alt="sci-cream Release 0.0.1" width="600"
style="display: block; margin: 0 auto;">

I have plans to continue adding functionality and expanding the knowledge base. Some highlights of
upcoming features from
[`TODO.md`](https://github.com/ramonrsv/sci-cream/blob/7539442/packages/sci-cream/TODO.md):

- Ice cream category (super premium, chocolate, sorbet, etc.) templates with acceptable ranges of
  key property values for each.
- Automatic balancing of recipes and sets of ingredients - preliminary support already in place.
- Quantifying and tracking the stabilization effects from different sources, including stabilizers,
  milk proteins, sugars, etc., and their effects on various properties, e.g. ice crystal
  suppression, viscosity, etc.

## Documentation and Blog

The [`sci-cream` library](https://docs.rs/sci-cream/latest/sci_cream/) contains a good foundational
knowledge base of ice cream science. It references formal sources like peer-reviewed literature, as
well as well-regarded resources like [Underbelly](/docs/resources#underbelly) and [Ice Cream
Science](/docs/resources#ice-cream-science). This [blog](/blog) and the [documentation](/docs) are a
place where I can share and collect more informal knowledge acquired throughout my ice cream
journey. The blog allows me to record relevant knowledge as I discover it, which may later be
incorporated into the documentation in a more structured fashion. Some upcoming posts:

- Herb (basil, mint, etc.) ice creams and issues with fiber fragments - need a cheesecloth, no
  matter how much you blend it.
- Chocolate ice creams and the importance of cocoa butter for that unique velvety texture.
- Nut butter ice creams, including how to make nut butters - I melted my Vitamix!
- Experiments quantifying the strengths of various stabilizers, mostly with regards to viscosity.
- Issues with lecithin - I've tried three, they all taste terrible! - and experiments with other
  emulsifiers that I have access to, e.g. gum arabic, polysorbate, and mono- and diglycerides.
